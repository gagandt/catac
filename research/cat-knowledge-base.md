# CAT (Common Admission Test) — Complete Knowledge Base

> Research compiled for the exam-prep tracker project. Source of truth for the CAT
> syllabus, pattern, scoring, resources, and strategy. Feeds `research/data/cat.json`.
> Last researched: July 2026. Pattern stable since CAT 2021 (2-hour, 3-section, 68 Q).

---

## 1. What CAT Is

- **Full name:** Common Admission Test.
- **Purpose:** Gateway exam for MBA / PGDM / PGP admission to the **21 IIMs** and
  **1,200+ other B-schools** in India (FMS Delhi, SPJIMR, MDI Gurgaon, IMT, IITs' DoMS,
  NITIE/IIM Mumbai, and many more accept CAT scores).
- **Conducted by:** The IIMs on a rotational basis. One IIM "convenes" each year.
- **Official website:** https://iimcat.ac.in — the **only** official site. Everything
  else is third-party coaching/aggregator content.
- **Frequency:** Once a year, on a **Sunday in late November**.
- **Mode:** Computer-Based Test (CBT) at test centers. Not online-from-home.

### Key dates & logistics (annual cycle)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Notification released | Late July |
| Registration window | Early August → ~mid/late September |
| Admit card | Early November |
| Exam day | Last Sunday of November (**CAT 2026: 29 Nov 2026**) |
| Results | First / second week of January |
| Slots | **3 slots** (morning / afternoon / evening), same day |
| Test cities | **170+** cities; choose up to **6** preferred cities |
| Application fee | **₹2,600** (General/EWS/NC-OBC); **₹1,300** (SC/ST/PwD) |

> Note: the convening IIM changes yearly (recent years: IIM Kozhikode, IIM Indore
> mentioned for the 2025/2026 cycles by trackers). Confirm on iimcat.ac.in when the
> notification drops — this is the one fact that must be pulled live each year.

### Eligibility

- Bachelor's degree with **≥ 50%** marks (or equivalent CGPA).
- **≥ 45%** for SC / ST / PwD categories.
- Final-year students (awaiting results) are eligible.
- **No age limit. No cap on number of attempts.**

## 2. Exam Pattern (Structure)

Stable format since 2021:

| # | Section | Questions | Marks | Time | MCQ | TITA (non-MCQ) |
|---|---------|-----------|-------|------|-----|----------------|
| 1 | **VARC** — Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension | 24 | 72 | 40 min | ~20–22 | ~2–4 |
| 2 | **DILR** — Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning | 22 | 66 | 40 min | ~12 | ~10 |
| 3 | **QA** — Quantitative Aptitude | 22 | 66 | 40 min | ~14 | ~8 |
| | **Total** | **68** | **204** | **120 min** | ~46 | ~22 |

### Rules that shape strategy

- **Sectional timing is locked.** 40 minutes per section, in fixed order (VARC → DILR
  → QA). You **cannot** move between sections or carry unused time forward.
- **PwD candidates** get 160 min total (53 min 20 sec per section).
- On-screen calculator (basic) provided. No physical calculator.

### Marking scheme

- **MCQ:** +3 correct, **−1 wrong**, 0 unattempted.
- **TITA (Type-In-The-Answer, non-MCQ):** +3 correct, **no negative marking**, 0 wrong.
- Implication: TITA questions are "free swings" — attempt all you can reason toward.
  On MCQs, blind guessing has negative expected value.

## 3. Detailed Syllabus (topic-by-topic)

CAT publishes **no official syllabus**. The list below is the de-facto syllabus
distilled from years of actual papers. This is the backbone of the app's topic tree.

### 3.1 VARC — Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (24 Q)

**Reading Comprehension (~16 Q, ~70% of section)** — the dominant sub-section.
- Passage types: argumentative, analytical, descriptive, narrative, abstract.
- Question types:
  - Main idea / central theme
  - Inference-based
  - Specific detail / fact identification
  - Author's tone, attitude, purpose
  - Logical structure of the passage
  - Vocabulary-in-context
  - Application / extrapolation

**Verbal Ability (~8 Q, ~30%)** — mostly **TITA**, mostly non-negative.
- **Para-jumbles** (~3 Q) — reorder 4–5 jumbled sentences.
- **Para-summary** (~3 Q) — pick the summary that best captures a paragraph.
- **Odd-sentence-out** (~2 Q) — remove the sentence that doesn't fit.
- **Para-completion** — supply the sentence that completes a paragraph.
- Sentence correction / grammar (rare in recent years).
- Analogies / word relationships (rare).

### 3.2 DILR — Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (22 Q)

Delivered as **4 sets of 5–6 questions each**. Set selection is the whole game —
you cannot solve all sets in 40 minutes.

**Data Interpretation (~half the sets)**
- Tables
- Bar graphs, line graphs
- Pie charts
- Venn diagrams
- Scatter plots, radar charts
- Caselets (data buried in a text passage)
- Mixed / multi-chart sets
- Data-sufficiency-style calculation sets

**Logical Reasoning (~half the sets)**
- Seating arrangements (linear, circular)
- Grid / matrix arrangements
- Blood relations, family trees
- Direction sense
- Syllogisms
- Coding-decoding
- Number & letter series, logical sequences
- Games & tournaments (scoring, knockouts)
- Clocks & calendars
- Routes & networks
- Cubes
- Constraint-based puzzles

### 3.3 QA — Quantitative Aptitude (22 Q)

Roughly Class 9–10 math, but twisted. **Arithmetic + Algebra ≈ 70%** of the section —
this is where prep effort pays off most.

**Arithmetic (~40%, highest yield)**
- Percentages
- Ratio & proportion
- Averages & mixtures / alligation
- Profit, loss & discount
- Simple & compound interest
- Time, speed & distance
- Time & work
- Pipes & cisterns
- Boats & streams
- Partnerships

**Algebra (~30%)**
- Linear equations
- Quadratic equations
- Inequalities & modulus
- Functions
- Logarithms
- Progressions (AP, GP, HP)
- Sequences & series
- Maxima & minima
- Polynomials

**Geometry & Mensuration (~15%)**
- Lines & angles
- Triangles & similarity
- Circles
- Quadrilaterals & polygons
- Coordinate geometry
- Trigonometry (basic)
- 2D & 3D mensuration (areas, volumes)

**Number System (~5–7%)**
- Divisibility & remainders
- Factors & multiples
- HCF & LCM
- Primes & composites
- Cyclicity, factorials
- Base systems
- Indices & surds

**Modern Math (~5–7%)**
- Permutations & combinations
- Probability
- Set theory & Venn diagrams
- Binomial theorem
- Functions & graphs

## 4. Scoring — Percentile, Normalization, Cutoffs

### How your raw score becomes a percentile

1. **Raw score** = sum of +3 / −1 across all questions.
2. **Normalization** across the 3 slots corrects for slot difficulty differences —
   nobody is advantaged/disadvantaged by which slot they sat. IIMs use top performers
   in each slot as anchors, then scale.
3. Normalized scaled score → **percentile** (your rank relative to all test-takers).

### Score → percentile (approximate, varies yearly with difficulty)

Numbers below are indicative. In a **moderately difficult** year:

| Percentile | Overall raw marks (approx) |
|------------|----------------------------|
| 90 %ile | ~52–60 |
| 95 %ile | ~60–70 |
| 98 %ile | ~70–80 |
| 99 %ile | ~80–96 |
| 99.5+ %ile | ~100–115+ |

(CAT 2024 reference points: ~57 → 90%ile, ~70 → 95%ile, ~96 → 99%ile. Harder papers
shift these lower; easier papers push them higher.)

### Sectional percentile → marks (CAT 2025 reference)

| Percentile | VARC | DILR | QA |
|------------|------|------|-----|
| 90 %ile | ~26 | ~16.7 | ~15 |
| 95 %ile | ~32.5 | ~21.5 | ~18.5 |
| 99 %ile | ~44 | ~29.8 | ~27.3 |

### IIM cutoffs — the reality

- **Top IIMs (A, B, C):** ~**99+ percentile** overall for General category; General
  Engineer Male (GEM) often needs **99.5+**. Roughly 110–120+ raw marks.
- **Older/newer IIMs:** 95–98 percentile bands.
- **Sectional cutoffs** matter: top schools enforce **80–90 percentile per section**.
  A high overall score with a weak section can still get you rejected. **VARC is the
  most common killer** for quant-strong engineers.
- **Admission is NOT percentile-only.** IIMs compute a **composite score**: CAT score
  + Class 10 % + Class 12 % + graduation % + work experience + academic diversity +
  gender diversity. Two people at the same percentile can get different call outcomes.
  Written Ability Test (WAT) + Group Discussion + Personal Interview follow shortlisting.

## 5. Preparation Strategy

### Timeline (6-month plan, adaptable)

| Phase | Months | Weekly hours | Focus |
|-------|--------|--------------|-------|
| **Foundation** | 1–2 | ~20 h | Concepts + fundamentals across all 3 sections; build daily reading habit |
| **Practice** | 3–4 | ~25–28 h | Topic-wise drilling, sectional tests, start mocks |
| **Revision + Mocks** | 5–6 | ~30–35 h | Full-length mocks + deep mock analysis, weak-area repair |

- Full-timers: **4–6 h/day.** Working professionals: **2–4 h/day.**
- **Consistency beats intensity.** Daily touch on all three sections.

### Section-specific

**VARC**
- **Read 5,000–7,000 words/day** of dense non-fiction: Aeon, The Economist, HBR,
  The Hindu editorials, longform essays. This is the single biggest lever.
- Practice all VA question types; RC is 70% of the section — prioritize it.
- Don't neglect VARC because it feels "unimprovable." Sectional cutoffs make it mandatory.

**DILR**
- **Set selection is the skill.** Spend the first 2–3 min scanning all 4 sets; pick the
  3–4 most approachable. Attempting the wrong set burns the section.
- Practice volume: ~50 assisted sets each of DI & LR → then ~300 self-solved sets each.
- Build a library of set "types" you recognize on sight.

**QA**
- Build **Arithmetic + Algebra** first (70% of the section). Spend 1–2 h/day.
- Master percentages/ratios early — they underlie most arithmetic.
- Learn shortcuts, but only after the concept is solid.

### Mock tests (the core engine)

- Take **25–35 full-length mocks** total.
- Cadence: 1 every 2 weeks early → 1–2 per week in the final 2–3 months.
- **Analysis > attempts.** For every mock, spend as long analyzing as taking it:
  which questions to skip, timing per section, silly errors, weak topics.

### Common failure modes

- Quant-strong candidates under-preparing VARC → miss sectional cutoff.
- Chasing raw attempts instead of accuracy/selection.
- Hoarding mocks without analyzing them.
- Starting VARC reading too late (reading speed compounds slowly).

## 6. Free Resources (curated)

CAT is one of the best-served exams for free prep. A self-study plan is fully viable.

### YouTube (concepts + strategy)

| Channel | Strength |
|---------|----------|
| **Rodha** | 1,400+ free videos across QA, DILR, VARC — most complete free course |
| **2IIM CAT Preparation** | In-depth tutorials, all sections; strong QA |
| **CATKing** | 1,000+ videos, full roadmap + OMET (other exams) coverage |
| **Cracku** | Lectures, shortcuts, mock analysis |
| **Anantha Chajjer** | QA shortcuts & concept clarity |
| **Shlok Bhagwat** | VARC / reading strategy |

### Free mocks & practice

- **Rodha** — 10 free mocks (3 full-length, 3 sectional, 6 topic-wise).
- **Cracku** — free mocks, question banks, previous-year papers.
- **CATKing** — free full-length mocks + topic quizzes.
- **iiM/official** — CAT releases **previous-year question papers + answer keys**;
  and each year publishes a **mock/tutorial** on iimcat.ac.in before the exam.

### Reading (for VARC)

- Aeon Essays, The Economist, Harvard Business Review, The Hindu / Indian Express
  editorials, longform journalism, Arts & Letters Daily.

### Practice material

- **Previous Year Questions (PYQs)** — the highest-signal practice. Available free from
  Cracku, 2IIM, CATKing, PagalGuy.
- Telegram groups / forums: **PagalGuy**, r/CATpreparation (Reddit) for peer strategy.
