## 1. What XAT Is

- **Full name:** Xavier Aptitude Test.
- **Purpose:** Gateway exam for MBA / PGDM admission to **XLRI Jamshedpur** and
  **160+ XAMI member/associate institutes** (XIMB/XUB, SPJIMR, IMT, TAPMI, GIM, KJ Somaiya,
  Great Lakes, and 250+ B-schools accept the score).
- **Conducted by:** **XLRI – Xavier School of Management, Jamshedpur**, on behalf of
  **XAMI (Xavier Association of Management Institutes)**.
- **Official website:** https://xatonline.in — the only official site.
- **Frequency:** Once a year, on the **first Sunday of January**.
- **Mode:** Computer-Based Test (CBT) at test centers.

### Key dates & logistics (annual cycle)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Registration window | Mid-July → early December |
| Admit card | Late December |
| Exam day | First Sunday of January (**XAT 2027: 3 Jan 2027, 2–5 PM**) |
| Results | Late January |
| Test cities | 70–110+ (Day-1 applicants get first-preference city) |
| Application fee | **₹2,300** base (XAT 2027) **+ ₹200 per XLRI programme** choice; late-fee tier higher |

> XAT 2027 registration opened **15 July 2026**. The exam is the first Sunday of January
> every year — confirm the exact date and fee on xatonline.in when the notification drops.

### Eligibility

- Recognized **bachelor's degree (≥ 3 years)**, any discipline.
- **Final-year students eligible** (must complete by June of the exam year).
- **No minimum marks. No age limit. No cap on attempts.**

## 2. Exam Pattern (Structure)

Stable format since XAT 2025 (unchanged for 2026 & 2027):

| Part | Section | Questions | Marks | Scored? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Part 1** (170 min, single combined timer, free movement) | **VA-LR** — Verbal & Logical Ability | 26 | 26 | ✅ percentile |
| | **DM** — Decision Making | 21 | 21 | ✅ percentile |
| | **QA-DI** — Quantitative Ability & Data Interpretation | 28 | 28 | ✅ percentile |
| **Part 2** (10 min, separate) | **GK** — General Knowledge | 20 | 20 | ❌ not in percentile |
| | **Total** | **95** | **95** | **scored portion = 75** |

### Rules that shape strategy

- **No sectional timing.** Part 1 is one 170-minute timer over VA-LR + DM + QA-DI with
  **free movement** between all three. Once Part 1 locks you enter the **separate 10-min GK**
  part and **cannot return**.
- **100% MCQ. Five options, exactly one correct. There is NO TITA** (unlike CAT).
- **GK is not counted** in the percentile / shortlisting score — it is used only at the
  **final XLRI selection stage** (WAT/GD/PI). No negative marking on GK, so attempt all 20.
- **No essay** since XAT 2025 (the Analytical Essay moved to the post-shortlist WAT stage).
- PwD candidates get +20 min per hour.

### Marking scheme

- **Correct: +1.** **Wrong: −0.25.**
- **Un-attempted penalty (XAT-unique):** the first **8** un-attempted questions in Part 1
  are free; **each blank beyond the 8th costs −0.10.** GK is exempt.
- This makes blind-skipping expensive: past 8 blanks, a considered guess (after eliminating
  options) usually beats a penalized blank.

### What changed recently

XAT 2024 → 2025 (carried into 2026/2027): duration 210 → **180 min**; Part 1 175 → **170**;
GK 30 → **10 min**, 25 → **20 Q**; **essay removed**; total 100(+essay) → **95 Q**.

## 3. Detailed Syllabus (reverse-engineered from recent papers)

### 3.1 VA-LR — Verbal & Logical Ability (~26 Q)

The XAT signature: it **kept the "old-school" verbal item types CAT abandoned** — direct
grammar, sentence correction, fill-in-the-blanks, standalone vocabulary, analogies, plus
**poetry** and **cartoon/image** questions — all as MCQs with negative marking.

- **Reading Comprehension (~15–17 Q, ~60%):** ~5 prose passages (2–4 Q each) + usually
  1 poetry passage. Style is **abstract/philosophical/literary** — harder to skim than CAT.
  Question types: main idea, tone/attitude/purpose, inference, detail, vocab-in-context,
  structure/function; plus poetry interpretation and a cartoon/image "main message" item.
- **Verbal Ability (~7–8 Q):** para-jumbles, **grammar & sentence correction (XAT-live)**,
  **fill-in-the-blanks/cloze (XAT-live)**, para-completion, para-summary,
  **vocabulary (XAT-live)**, analogies.
- **Critical & Logical Reasoning (~3 Q):** strengthen/weaken, assumptions, conclusion/
  inference, cause-effect, statement-argument/course-of-action. XAT LR is argument-based —
  grid/seating/blood-relation puzzles are **not** an XAT staple.

### 3.2 DM — Decision Making (~21 Q) — UNIQUE TO XAT

Exists on no other major MBA exam. ~7 caselets × ~3 Q. No syllabus, no formulas — pure
managerial judgment. Case types: **ethical dilemmas** (heavy), **business/strategy**,
**HR/people conflict**, **financial**, and occasional **data-based/analytical** DM (XAT
2024/2025 were largely qualitative). Carries one of XLRI's highest sectional cutoffs.

**The XLRI "expected-answer" philosophy — what makes an option correct:**
- **Balanced / win-win / stakeholder-inclusive** — reconciles all parties, harms the fewest.
- **Ethical and fair** — reject dishonest, exploitative, or purely-profit options even if profitable.
- **Avoid extremes** — the harshest ("fire/sue/shut it down") and the most passive
  ("do nothing") options are usually traps; the moderate constructive middle wins.
- **Long-term / sustainable** over short-term fixes.
- **Rational & practical** — grounded only in what the caselet states; import no outside
  assumptions and no personal opinion. Answer as a neutral professional manager.

### 3.3 QA-DI — Quantitative Ability & Data Interpretation (~28 Q)

Roughly QA ~15–16 + DI ~12–13. Toughest, make-or-break section; a good attempt is low
(~9–10 correct can fetch ~95 percentile in a hard year).

**How XAT QA differs from CAT:** tougher, wider **geometry** and — unlike CAT — it **tests
trigonometry**; more **pure math** (logs, functions, progressions); conceptual & **PYQ-heavy**
(XAT recycles patterns, so past papers are the best prep); DI is calculation-heavy and can
be unusual (scatter plots, number-matrix, time-zone).

- **Arithmetic (largest bucket):** percentages, ratio, averages/mixtures/alligation,
  profit-loss-discount, SI/CI, time-speed-distance, time-work, partnership.
- **Algebra:** linear/quadratic, inequalities/modulus, functions & graphs, progressions
  (AP/GP/HP), logs/surds/indices, maxima-minima.
- **Geometry & Mensuration:** triangles, circles, polygons/quadrilaterals, coordinate
  geometry, **trigonometry**, mensuration 2D/3D.
- **Number System:** HCF/LCM, divisibility/factors, remainders, base systems, integer properties.
- **Modern Math:** P&C, probability, set theory / Venn.
- **Data Interpretation:** tables, bar/line, pie, caselet DI, scatter/unusual charts, data sufficiency.

### 3.4 GK — General Knowledge (~20 Q)

No negative marking; **excluded from percentile/shortlist**, used only at final selection.
~60% current affairs / ~40% static, with an **XAT business/economy tilt**.
- **Static:** business/corporate (founders, brands, HQs), economics/finance (RBI, IMF/WTO),
  polity/Constitution, history, geography, awards, books/authors.
- **Current affairs (last 6–12 months):** business & deals, national/international events,
  awards/appointments, sports, science/tech/AI, government schemes & budget.

## 4. Scoring — Percentile & Cutoffs

Scoring model: **percentile bands** (like CAT). Scored out of **75** (GK excluded). Raw
scores are famously low and swing with paper difficulty; XLRI publishes no official table.

### Score → percentile (overall, approximate)

| Percentile | Raw marks / 75 |
|---|---|
| 90 | ~23–27 |
| 95 | ~30–33 |
| 98 | ~33–35 |
| 99 | **~35–40** |
| 99.5 | ~39–42 |

Easy year (2023) needed ~43–46 for 99; a hard year ~33–36. QA-DI is the steepest (needs
~15+ for 99); DM percentiles inflate fastest (~13 ≈ 96–97).

### XLRI cutoffs (XAT 2026 official)

- **PGDM-BM:** Male ≥ **94** overall (QA ≥85, VALR ≥82, DM ≥82); Female/3rd ≥ 88. Sectional
  + overall both enforced.
- **PGDM-HRM:** ~90–92 overall (weights VALR & DM higher, QA lower); Female/3rd 84–87.
- **Practical target:** 96–98+ overall with balanced sections for a safe XLRI BM call.

### Other schools (typical overall percentile)

- **90+:** SPJIMR, XIMB/XUB, IMT Ghaziabad/Hyderabad
- **85+:** KJ Somaiya, GIM Goa, FORE
- **80+:** TAPMI, Great Lakes, IMI Delhi, MICA, LIBA, IRMA
- **75+:** BIMTECH, Welingkar, XIME

**Post-test:** percentile → institute shortlist → XLRI stage = WAT/essay + GD + PI; GK
feeds the final composite, not the percentile.

## 5. Preparation Strategy

Part 1 (QA/VA/LRDI) overlaps ~80% with CAT — most aspirants build the base during CAT prep
(through late Nov), then pivot in the **~5–6 weeks between CAT and XAT** to what's XAT-specific:
**Decision Making** and **full-length XAT mocks** (different clock + un-attempt penalty).

### Section-specific

- **VA-LR:** RC is ~60% — prioritize it. Revive grammar/vocab/FIB/analogies/poems/cartoons
  that CAT dropped. Everything is MCQ with −0.25 → accuracy first.
- **DM (highest ROI):** no syllabus; a fresher can reach 90+ percentile in 1–2 weeks of PYQ
  practice. Apply the XLRI philosophy above; ~10–11 of 21 correct is already strong. Practice
  **PYQs, not mocks** — official past-paper keys reflect XLRI's real logic; coaching-mock DM
  keys are subjective and can mistrain you.
- **QA-DI (make-or-break):** concept + PYQ over speed. Cover geometry + trig (XAT-specific).
- **GK:** attempt all 20 (no negative marking) but don't over-invest — a monthly compilation
  + business headlines suffice; it doesn't affect the percentile.

### The un-attempt-penalty lever

First 8 blanks in Part 1 are free; beyond that −0.10 each; a wrong answer is −0.25. Track
the blank count live: attempt only confident questions on the first pass (protect accuracy),
then near the end guess-fill (after eliminating options) down to ≤8 blanks. The allowance is
**one pooled budget across all of Part 1**, not per section.

### Time strategy (Part 1, no sectional timer)

Common high-scorer split: DM 30–40 min, VA-LR ~50 min, QA-DI ~55–60 min, then GK ~10 min.
Start with your strongest section to bank marks; first-pass the sitters, flag the rest, return.

### Common failure modes

- Bringing personal opinion/emotion into DM and picking the extreme option.
- Trusting coaching-mock DM keys over official PYQ keys.
- Ignoring the un-attempt penalty (over-skip → −0.10 bleed, or panic-attempt → −0.25 bleed).
- Treating XAT like CAT — skipping official XAT mocks and under-practicing DM.
- Over-investing in GK.
- Under-practicing PYQs — XAT (esp. DM and QA) repeats patterns; the last 7–10 papers are the best edge.
